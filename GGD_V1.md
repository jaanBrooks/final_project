Title: The Freshmen 15

Game Summary: 
Time and speedrunning based platformer where trying to manage the stresses of Freshmen year at University are first and foremost. You must maintain a good GPA, turn in assignments or get to finals on time, and of course avoid gaining the ubiquitous Freshmen 15. 

Inspiration: 

Personal Inspiration
I wanted to make a game for which the average college student and alumni could relate to, and I plan on doing this through a game which exemplifies and embodies the stresses of Freshmen year. As someone who has stressed about grades, making sure I get to my scheduled events on time, and also taking care of my health, I really wanted to incorporate these challenges into my game. 

Gameplay inspiration:
Speedrunners is a video game which me and my friends in my townhouse have been playing almost daily for weeks now. It is a local couch co-op game where the objective is to outrun your opponents on a cyclical map. You gain advantages by using items, performing platforming tactics at opportune times, such as sliding, grapping, double jumping and sprinting and also choosing the better path when multiple are available. This creates a very engaging game where unlike most platformers where it doesn't really matter how you get to the end as long as you make it there, Speedrunners emphasizes attention to detail and trying to maximize your route. One could make the argument this relates to college, where it's not just about graduation, it's about maximizing your experience along the way which is why I think thematically it is a great source of inspiration. Gameplay wise it is also incredibly addicting and satisfying to perfect your route which is something I think would be so cool to emulate. 

Concept
Gameplay overiview:
Hero: Meet 18 year old college freshmen Timmy. He is excited for the upcoming year, but is also a little anxious about keeping up with his responsibilities.

Setting: The Freshmen 15 takes place in Upstate University. 

Actions to explore: 

~Getting from one place to another, example given say your dorm to office hours to hand in an assignment, getting coffee along the way, rock climbing (wall jumping), getting good grades and eating right.


Emotions: The player should empathize with timmy, and inherently feel his stress from managing grades, responsibilities and health. 

Theme interpretation:

Action code: I think game should start off with Timmy receiving his acceptance to upstate university. It will have been his dream school and as an audience we can resonate with the passion and anxiety that comes with receiving a life altering opportunity. 

Enigma code: The subject is of course timmy's academic journey. The mystery of course is whether or not he will be able to pass freshmen year. To incorporate a delay stage, I think it is crucial timmy "fails" his first test. Maybe the end of the first level is him getting to the test taking area then dialogue explains he fails. The audience thinks, does timmy really have what it takes. For the "jamming" portion, I think it would appropriate for timmy to have to attend office hours and submit his retake to see if his professor will still let him pass. The engima will finally be solved when upon successfully making it to say to the end of the second level to hand in his test retake and the professor grades it and he passes.

I want the game's look and feel to convey a few things. For look, I want the backgroud to cycle fast to convey feelings like your in a rush and time is flying by which will be helped by the countdown timer, which relates to how college feels like a frenzy rush. I also want the atmosphere to be like that of a college campus, one that is green and pretty to juxtapose the stress and pace of the game which correlates to my personal experience at university where my beautiful campus helped oppose the stress of academics. 

Game mechanics:
In the first level, the player will first be introduced to the movement mechanics. It will be a sort of tutorial level, called get to college, where basically before introducing the more advanced features the player simply learns to move properly.

Movement mechanics to learn in the first level:

1.Variable jump height: holding jump makes you jump higher

2.Sliding: allows the player to decrease height to fit into narrow gaps

3.Wall Jumping: run into walls and jump that are marked a certain way such that you can wall jump off of them (maybe rock climbing wall as thats an activity i do at college)

4.Sprint Boost: Running through a coffee stand will give you a dash boost to bank, hold down shift to utilize it

Level two mechanics:
The player will finally be at college where we can introduce a few new mechanics:

GPA meter: 
meant to incentivize the player to be tactical with their movement: take paths and collect good grades smartly and avoid bad grades, else if your gpa reaches a failing grade GAME OVER

Freshmen 15 meter: 
player will gain weight and or lose weight with respect to collecting certain consumables: weight gainers will be beer and donuts with potential for more later

Player will lose weight with respect to things like carrots, avocados, chicken breast

The fatter you are the slower you are which is critical because it will affect your ability to make certain jumps and get to the endpoint on time

Time meter: get to your specfic goal (midterm hall or office hours) before the time runs out, time runnning out is a game over condition

1st level start with getting accepted to college and then get to college learn basic mechanics

2nd level
Get to midterm on time and take it with new GPA and freshmen 15 mechanics
At the end, learn that you failed the midterm but you have a take home retake

3rd level:
Final level, submit your retake to your professor and pass freshmen fall.
